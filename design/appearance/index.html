Evolution of Cybersecurity Matters


One of the challenges describing topics in cybersecurity is illustrating a hacker. There isn't a hard and fast stereotype to characterize a masked figure behind a computer screen. I created a simple drawing to resemble a hacker, a malicious internet user. The drawing is resembled after an anonymous figure with a fedora hat. One of the drawing included glasses, akin to a mysterious figure popularized in computer hacking related movies in the 1900s.

How I emulated my models:
The color model I used focused on a dark navy blue background contrasted with white and orange foreground colors. For the text, I interplayed all types of colors against a contrasting background.

First Iteration

Critique #1

My first critic pointed out that the icon represented a suspicious figure, correlating to a hacker. The glasses did not affect much of the appearance. She felt the background of the first variant (with the code background) added to the context of hacking and blended well with the opacity amount. She preferred the first one since the additional features of the icon reinforces the anonymity concept.

Revisions based on this critique

Critique #2

It wasn't apparent to him immediately as to what the figure was since the curve around the ears were not as definitive, and the position of the glasses were a little off. The text and color scheme had a very techy feel to it and helped convey the overall idea. He preferred the more minimalist approach of the second figure since the coding background in the first was a little distracting.

Revisions based on this critique

Critique #3

My third critic pointed out immediately the shape of a detective, mysterious figure since the full silhouette adds to the anonymity. The background coding text in the first image further reinforced the concept. She preferred the first over the second primarily due to the inverted color combination with the coding text in the background. She also felt that the placement for both variants are asymmetrical and well laid out.

Revisions based on this critique
